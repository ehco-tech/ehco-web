EHCO Curated Editorial

Last edited: December 15, 2025

Formation and Highly Anticipated Debut

The journey to BABYMONSTER's creation began years before their official debut. YG Entertainment trademarked the name in late 2019, signaling their plans for the agency's first girl group since BLACKPINK's 2016 debut.1 The group was carefully crafted through years of rigorous training and evaluations. Members trained extensively—some for up to six years—before being selected for the final lineup.2

YG Entertainment built anticipation through a strategic rollout, announcing the group's formation on December 30, 2022, with a poster labeled "YG Next Movement." The announcement video released on New Year's Day 2023 garnered an impressive 15 million views within three days and featured appearances by label legends including members of BLACKPINK, WINNER, and AKMU.3 This debut announcement even contributed to a surge in the company's stock price, which jumped by nearly 12 percent.3

The seven members were gradually revealed through live performance videos throughout early 2023, showcasing their vocal abilities and stage presence. The group competed in the reality show "Last Evaluation" to determine the final lineup, which was officially announced on May 11, 2023.4 However, the debut faced unexpected changes when member Ahyeon went on hiatus for health reasons just before the scheduled November launch.5

Record-Breaking Debut Era

BABYMONSTER made their debut on November 27, 2023, as a six-member group with the digital single "Batter Up"—without Ahyeon, who remained on health hiatus.6 The debut single immediately shattered records, achieving a remarkable 22.59 million views within 24 hours, making it the most-viewed K-pop debut song in a single day.7 The momentum continued as the video reached 50 million views in four days, 100 million in 18 days, and ultimately hit 200 million views in just 53 days—the fastest K-pop debut to reach this milestone.8

The group's YouTube channel demonstrated equally impressive growth, surpassing one million subscribers in just 52 days since its creation and reaching two million subscribers in 129 days.3 This rapid subscriber growth positioned them as one of the fastest-growing K-pop girl groups on the platform, earning them the nickname "YouTube Queens."9

On February 1, 2024, BABYMONSTER released their pre-release track "Stuck in the Middle," which quickly surpassed 20 million views in a single day, continuing their streak of breaking viewership records.10 These early releases demonstrated the immense anticipation surrounding the group and established their presence as major contenders in the K-pop industry.

Complete Lineup and Official Debut

The group's trajectory took a significant turn when Ahyeon officially rejoined in early 2024. YG Entertainment confirmed on January 25, 2024, that Ahyeon had returned to the company and would begin promoting with the complete group.11 This reunion marked what many considered the group's true debut as BABYMONSTER released their first mini album "BABYMONS7ER" on April 1, 2024, featuring all seven members for the first time.12

The EP featured the hit title track "SHEESH," which became their first top-ten hit on South Korea's Circle Digital Chart.13 The album also included "Like That," co-written by global superstar Charlie Puth, showcasing the group's international connections.14 The music video for "SHEESH" debuted at number one on YouTube's global music video ranking and has since accumulated nearly 310 million views.14

The success of "BABYMONS7ER" was substantial, with first-week sales reaching 401,287 copies. The album broke the first-week sales record for a K-pop girl group debut, and YG Entertainment anticipated even higher figures once overseas exports were fully counted.15 The track "SHEESH" demonstrated staying power on charts, remaining on the Billboard Global Chart for 18 consecutive weeks and on the Billboard 200 Chart for 14 weeks.14

Chart Domination with "Drip"

In November 2024, BABYMONSTER released their first full-length album "Drip," marking what YG Entertainment described as the quickest turnaround for releasing a full album following a rookie group's debut.16 The album featured nine tracks, including double title tracks "DRIP" and "CLIK CLAK." Notably, the title track "Drip" was co-produced by legendary K-pop artist G-Dragon, adding significant prestige to the release.17

The album achieved unprecedented success for the group. First-week sales reached 677,961 copies—a 1.7-fold increase from their debut album. Total orders within the first week approximated 820,000 copies, nearly doubling the performance of their previous release.18 The album became BABYMONSTER's first entry on the Billboard 200, debuting at number 149, and the title track "Drip" achieved career-high positions on global charts, reaching number 16 on the Billboard Global Excl. U.S. chart and number 30 on the Billboard Global 200.19

Beyond the United States, "Drip" topped iTunes Album Charts in 14 countries and reached number two on the iTunes Worldwide Album Chart.20 In China, the album dominated major QQ Music charts, holding top positions for 13 consecutive days across multiple categories including Rising, Trending, Popular, and New Song charts.21 The success culminated in June 2025 when "Drip" surpassed one million cumulative sales on the Hanteo Chart, marking BABYMONSTER's first million-seller record approximately seven months after its release.18

Record-Breaking World Tour

BABYMONSTER launched their first world tour, "Hello Monsters," on January 25-26, 2025, with a two-day concert at KSPO Dome in Seoul that drew an estimated 20,000 attendees.22 This achievement was particularly remarkable as they became the fastest female act to headline the venue, accomplishing this feat within just eight months of their debut.3

The tour's momentum continued impressively in Japan, where all 100,000 tickets were sold, prompting the addition of restricted view seats and extra shows to accommodate overwhelming demand. Across ten shows in the country, BABYMONSTER attracted 150,000 attendees, setting a record as the fastest K-pop girl group to achieve such attendance numbers in Japan.23 At K-Arena Yokohama alone, the group performed for 55,000 fans over three days, with the originally scheduled two shows selling out all 40,000 seats before a special event-style performance was added.24

The tour expanded across Asia and North America, featuring performances in multiple countries including the United States, Canada, Singapore, Malaysia, Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia, and Taiwan. The entire tour spanned 32 dates and attracted approximately 300,000 attendees in total.25 During performances, the group showcased their versatility with hit songs, solo stages for each member, and even covers of their YG Entertainment predecessors 2NE1, demonstrating both their technical prowess and respect for the label's legacy.

Continued Evolution and Recent Releases

Throughout 2024 and 2025, BABYMONSTER continued releasing new music that showcased their artistic growth. In July 2024, they released the digital single "Forever," which combined hip-hop elements with fresh vocals for a summery comeback. The music video became YouTube's second most-viewed video within 24 hours, amassing over 5 million views in under eight hours.26 The song "SHEESH" also earned recognition by ranking fifth on Spotify's global K-pop impact list for the first half of 2024.27

In 2025, the group faced a temporary setback when member Rami took a hiatus for health reasons, missing the release of their pre-release single "Hot Sauce" in July 2025. The song paid homage to Seo Taiji and Boys' 1992 debut with its late-1980s hip-hop sound and featured visuals styled after the pioneering trio's original album font.28 Despite performing as a six-member ensemble, the group maintained their momentum.

BABYMONSTER released their second EP "We Go Up" on October 10, 2025, featuring the lead single of the same name along with four new songs.29 The release reinforced YG Entertainment's commitment to the group's continued growth through 2026 and beyond. Industry professionals and media outlets have recognized BABYMONSTER as one of the "Next Generation Leaders" and named them among the "Future of Music" artists by Rolling Stone Korea in 2024, cementing their status as a pivotal force in the next generation of K-pop.9
