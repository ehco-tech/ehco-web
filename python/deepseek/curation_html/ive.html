Defining Fourth-Generation Excellence

When Starship Entertainment announced plans to debut a new girl group in the second half of 2021‚Äîtheir first since WJSN in 2016‚Äîindustry attention was inevitable. The label had confirmed that former IZ*ONE members Jang Wonyoung and An Yujin would be part of the lineup, generating immediate buzz around the project. In November 2021, official social media accounts launched under the name IVE, with members revealed sequentially between November 3 and 8.1

The group name carries intentional weight. "IVE is short for 'I Have' and conveys a message that the band is well-prepared and is ready to get their feet in the door by showing what they have," Jang Wonyoung explained during their debut showcase. The philosophy positioned IVE not as a group growing toward completeness, but as one arriving fully formed‚Äîa "complete girl group" from inception.2

Six Paths to Starship: The Origin Stories

An Yujin, born September 1, 2003 in Cheongju, realized her idol aspirations after attending a year-end ceremony in Seoul with friends. The then-aspiring trainee began sending emails to entertainment companies, attaching singing clips and photos. Starship Entertainment responded with an audition invitation, and remarkably, she was the only candidate at her session. "The staff told me only I would be auditioning," Yujin later recalled. She joined the company as a trainee in 2016, ultimately representing Starship on Mnet's Produce 48 in 2018 alongside fellow trainees Jang Wonyoung and Cho Ka-hyeon. Finishing fifth, she debuted with project group IZ*ONE on October 29, 2018.3

Jang Wonyoung entered the entertainment industry through chance encounter. At just twelve years old, she was attending her older sister Jang Da-ah's middle school graduation ceremony when a Starship casting agent spotted her. The scout was struck by her "tall stature and cute face." After approximately fourteen months of training, she competed on Produce 48, where her commanding stage presence and youthful charisma earned her first place‚Äîmaking her the youngest center in the show's history at fourteen. As IZ*ONE's center, she balanced intense promotional schedules with home-schooling, passing qualification exams with perfect scores in Korean, English, and Mathematics.4

Gaeul (Kim Gaeul, born September 24, 2002) was discovered through her passion for dance. In April 2017, during her third year of middle school, she competed in the preliminary round of the Incheon Youth Dance Competition. Unknown to her, Starship Entertainment scouts were watching. "At the time, she had no idea agency scouts were watching," records indicate. However, this was not her first encounter with industry interest‚Äîanother agency had previously approached her, but her parents rejected the offer, making a deal that if she were scouted again at a future competition, they would support her dreams. When Starship extended their offer, fate aligned. Gaeul trained for approximately 4.5 years‚Äîthe longest of any IVE member‚Äîand notably shared trainee periods with both Yujin and Wonyoung before their Produce 48 departure.5

Rei (Naoi Rei, born February 3, 2004 in Nagoya, Aichi Prefecture, Japan) represents Starship's first Japanese artist. Her love for K-pop was cultivated through her mother, a TVXQ fan who introduced her to Korean music. Rei developed particular admiration for Red Velvet, especially member Joy. In April 2018, at fourteen, she participated in the Loen Friends Global Audition in Japan, performing BLACKPINK's "Whistle" and Red Velvet's "Happiness." Her distinct vocal tone caught the judges' attention, earning her a trainee position. One month later, she moved to Seoul alone, committing to learn Korean without translation tools during training. After 3.5 years, she debuted as IVE's main rapper and only foreign member.6

Liz (Kim Ji-won, born November 21, 2004) hails from Jeju Island, where she was active in her school's dance club and athletics. Her K-pop awakening came through Girls' Generation's Taeyeon, who became her role model. In December 2017, before joining any agency, Liz appeared in Taeyeon's "This Christmas" music video, portraying the singer's younger self‚Äîthough she was reportedly too shy to say anything beyond "I'm a fan" upon meeting her idol. In summer 2019, she auditioned at Starship's Pepsi Summer Open Audition. According to a member of Starship's rookie development team, Liz's vocal talent and visuals were "overwhelming," and her eagerness during introduction impressed despite her naturally shy personality. She moved to Seoul with her mother and younger brother to pursue training.7

Leeseo (Lee Hyun-seo, born February 21, 2007) entered the entertainment sphere earliest among the members. In 2016, during her second year of elementary school, she won the SM Entertainment Kids Model Contest, becoming part of SM Kids' first generation‚Äîselected from approximately 400 candidates. Three years later, in 2019, a pivotal encounter occurred: while shopping with her parents at Shinsegae Department Store's Gangnam branch, sixth-grader Leeseo went to buy a drink and was spotted by a Starship Entertainment director. Her "eye-catching appearance" led to an immediate audition invitation. She trained for approximately 2.5 years before debuting at age fourteen as IVE's maknae.8

"Eleven": A Record-Breaking Debut

On December 1, 2021, IVE released their debut single album "Eleven." The title track, a pop-dance number exploring how affection deepens between lovers, featured lyrics by Seo Ji-eum‚Äîknown for work with EXO and Red Velvet. "Best of the best players in soccer are usually categorized as 'best 11,' and the same idea applies to our group," Wonyoung explained of the title's significance. The commercial response exceeded expectations: within its first week, the album sold 152,229 copies‚Äîthe highest first-week sales for a girl group debut album in 2021.2

On December 8‚Äîexactly seven days after debut‚ÄîIVE earned their first music show win on MBC M's Show Champion, setting a new record for the fastest first win by a girl group in K-pop history, surpassing ITZY's previous nine-day record with "Dalla Dalla" (2019). "Eleven" ultimately secured thirteen music show trophies, including triple crowns on KBS2's Music Bank, MBC's Music Core, and SBS' Inkigayo. The track reached number nine on Billboard's World Digital Song Sales and charted on the Billboard Global 200 for fourteen consecutive weeks.9

2022: "Love Dive" and the Daesang Sweep

IVE's sophomore release solidified their position as the year's breakthrough act. On April 5, 2022, they released their second single album "Love Dive," which charted at number one on the Circle Digital Chart‚Äîtheir first chart-topping song in South Korea. The track peaked at number ten on Billboard's Global Excl. US chart, charting for twenty-nine consecutive weeks. Within a week of release, "Love Dive" had spawned a TikTok challenge that accumulated 200 million views.10

The song's impact extended through year-end award season. "Love Dive" earned Song of the Year (Daesang) at four major ceremonies: the MAMA Awards, Melon Music Awards, Golden Disc Awards, and Asia Artist Awards. IVE became only the fourth K-pop group in history to win both a rookie award and a Daesang in the same year, following 2NE1, Miss A, and aespa. South Korean music webzine IZM Critic ranked it as the best K-pop song of 2022, praising its "universal reason and long-standing sense of success in music art."11

Just four months later, IVE returned with "After Like," released August 22, 2022. The disco-influenced track sampled Gloria Gaynor's 1978 anthem "I Will Survive," creating an instantly recognizable hook. "After Like" achieved IVE's first Perfect All-Kill‚Äîa rare music chart achievement where a song simultaneously tops real-time, daily, and weekly charts across all major South Korean streaming platforms. The song claimed fourteen music show wins, more than any other track that year. The accompanying single album sold over 1.7 million copies.12

Global Expansion and "I've IVE"

IVE's trajectory continued upward through 2023. In February, they embarked on their first solo tour, "The Prom Queens," with six sold-out dates across Seoul, Yokohama, and Kobe. The Asia tour leg later attracted 97,000 spectators across six stops in Southeast Asia and Taiwan. On March 24, Starship announced IVE had signed with Columbia Records for US promotion, facilitated through parent company Kakao Entertainment's partnership with the label.13

Their first studio album, "I've IVE," arrived on April 10, 2023. The pre-release single "Kitsch," dropped March 27, debuted at number one on the Circle Digital Chart and achieved IVE's second Perfect All-Kill. The album itself featured significant member participation‚Äîseven of eleven songs included songwriting credits from the members. Rei, in particular, emerged as a prolific contributor, writing rap lyrics in Korean, Japanese, and English for tracks including "After Like," "Kitsch," "Royal," and "Hypnosis." At the 2023 Melon Music Awards, "I've IVE" won Album of the Year.14

2024-2025: Five-Time Million Sellers

IVE's commercial dominance continued with their second extended play, "IVE SWITCH," released April 29, 2024. In August 2024, they performed at Lollapalooza Chicago, beginning what would become a groundbreaking festival run. Their third EP, "IVE EMPATHY," arrived February 3, 2025, preceded by pre-release track "Rebel Heart" on January 13. The album sold over 1.04 million copies in its first week, making IVE five-time million sellers. "Rebel Heart" achieved a Perfect All-Kill and claimed eleven music show wins, while title track "Attitude" secured four additional trophies‚Äîfifteen wins total from a single album cycle.15

Billboard recognized "Rebel Heart" among "The 25 Best K-Pop Songs of 2025 (So Far): Critic's Picks," praising the song's "rich vocal harmonies, uplifting chorus, and lush soundscape that blends emotional strings with uptempo drums." The track spent ten consecutive weeks on Billboard's Global Excl. US chart and accumulated over 57 million Spotify streams. In July 2025, IVE performed at both Lollapalooza Berlin and Lollapalooza Paris, becoming the first K-pop girl group to appear at Lollapalooza in three different cities over two consecutive years. At Paris, they delivered a dynamic 55-minute set with live band accompaniment, with songs like "Rebel Heart" drawing massive Korean sing-alongs from French audiences.16

Throughout their career, IVE has maintained a public commitment to social causes. In February 2023, the group and Starship donated ‚Ç©150 million to the Hope Bridge National Disaster Relief Association following the devastating earthquakes in Turkey and Syria. In March 2025, they contributed an additional ‚Ç©200 million for wildfire relief efforts in South Korea. Their artistic identity, centered on the message that "true love begins with loving oneself," has evolved from self-confidence into empathy for others‚Äîa thematic expansion reflected in their 2025 album title.17
