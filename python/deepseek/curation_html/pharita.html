From Child Model to K-Pop Trainee

Pharita Boonpakdeethaveeyod was born on August 26, 2005, in Bangkok, Thailand, into a business-oriented family. Her mother runs a local flower shop, while her father works in the finance sector.1 Growing up in Bangkok's international community, Pharita attended Ruamrudee International School, one of Thailand's most prestigious educational institutions known for producing top students and sending dozens to medical schools annually.2

Before pursuing K-pop, Pharita established herself as a successful child model in Thailand. At just 13 years old in 2018, she won the title of Teen Model Thailand, along with victories at Talent Kids & Teen 2018 and Asia Junior Fashion Runway 2018.3 Her modeling achievements extended internationally‚Äîshe secured the Grand Prize and Champion title at the 2019 International Fashion Exhibition in China, earned a silver medal at the 2019 Asia Pacific Arts Festival, and even claimed the Junior Street Dance Champion title at the Idol World Dance Cup in South Korea.3

On February 29, 2020, Pharita was introduced as contestant GI3218 on the Thai survival show "Idol Paradise."4 However, she departed the program before it officially aired‚Äîa decision that would prove pivotal. In July 2020, at age 14, Pharita auditioned for YG Entertainment's global auditions, performing BLACKPINK's "How You Like That."5 Her audition stood out dramatically: she was selected from an astonishing 1,226 applicants, representing one of the most competitive acceptance rates in K-pop trainee recruitment.6

Disney Visuals and Four Years of Training

After passing the audition, Pharita made the bold decision to leave her family in Thailand and move to South Korea alone to pursue her dreams as a YG Entertainment trainee.5 Her training period would span four years‚Äîfrom 2020 to 2024‚Äîduring which she developed her vocal abilities, dance skills, and Korean language proficiency.

On February 13, 2023, YG Entertainment released Pharita's introduction video, titled "BABYMONSTER ‚Äì Introducing PHARITA," making her the second member to receive a dedicated feature after Ruka.7 The video showcased her covering songs including "What Other People Say" by Sam Fischer and Demi Lovato, "Congratulations" by Demi Lovato, and "Believer" by Imagine Dragons, while also highlighting her modeling background and guitar-playing ability.8

Yang Hyun-suk, YG Entertainment's founder and executive producer, made a memorable observation about Pharita's appearance, stating that she looked like "she came straight out of a Disney movie" due to her photogenic qualities and unrivaled visuals.7 In the introduction video, Yang expressed both concern and optimism: "I felt really sorry and was worried about PHARITA because she had to leave her family behind and come to this foreign country, but she's started to gain confidence these days and has been smiling a lot. Once she gets used to the country more, she'll spread her wings widely."7

The "All of Me" Performance

During the "Last Evaluation" series that determined BABYMONSTER's final lineup, Pharita delivered a standout solo performance covering John Legend's "All of Me." The clean version of her performance was uploaded to BABYMONSTER's official YouTube channel on April 8, 2023, following Episode 5 of the series.9 Her rendition showcased her dreamy, pure vocal tone and emotional expressiveness that captivated audiences despite her young age.3

YG Entertainment's main producer Choi Hyun-suk praised Pharita's breath control and ability to maintain a stable tone, describing her performance as "consistent" during the vocal evaluation aired on Last Evaluation.13 Her vocal technique demonstrated her capability to hit deep low notes while effortlessly reaching high notes throughout the chorus‚Äîa versatility unusual for someone born in 2005.3 Within BABYMONSTER's lineup, Pharita specializes in hooks and high notes, possessing a high, resonant, and bright vocal tone that complements the group's overall sound.1

Debut and Lisa's Blessing

On May 11, 2023, BABYMONSTER's final seven-member lineup was announced, with Pharita ranking fifth in the evaluation results.5 This achievement made her the third Thai idol to debut under YG Entertainment, following BLACKPINK's Lisa and her fellow BABYMONSTER member Chiquita.10

The connection between Pharita and Lisa extended beyond simply being labelmates. Lisa appeared in Pharita's introduction video and, along with Chiquita, dined with Lisa at a Thai restaurant where the BLACKPINK member gave them her signed CDs.5 More significantly, Lisa although did not mention Pharita's name but provding a hint saying "There is a Thai trainee that reminds me of when I was young. I‚Äôm trying to take care of her" in a Rolling Stone interview, providing a public endorsement that carried substantial weight in the K-pop industry.12 Pharita has consistently cited BLACKPINK, and Lisa specifically, as her role models, expressing her wish to follow in Lisa's footsteps one day.5

BABYMONSTER debuted as a six-member group on November 27, 2023, with the digital single "Batter Up," as member Ahyeon took a health hiatus.11 On April 1, 2024, the group officially debuted as a complete seven-member ensemble with their first mini-album "BABYMONS7ER."11 The EP received over 460,000 pre-orders and debuted at number three on the Circle Album Chart, with title track "SHEESH" eventually peaking at number ten on the Circle Digital Chart.6

Rising Recognition and Challenges

Throughout 2024, Pharita's live vocal performances on music shows like M Countdown and Inkigayo drew attention from Korean media outlets including OSEN, Newsen, and Star News, which praised her stable high notes and breath control during demanding choreography. Her multilingual abilities‚Äîfluency in Thai and English, with near-fluency in Korean achieved in just over two years of training‚Äîpositioned her as BABYMONSTER's English spokesperson and international representative.

However, Pharita's journey wasn't without obstacles. In March 2023, when BABYMONSTER's official lineup was announced, she faced criticism on Korean online forums regarding visual standards and her suitability for debut, with some viewers questioning her aura for a vocal position.1 During the group's promotional tour in Japan in May-June 2024, she suffered a minor wrist injury that required modifications to her choreography in certain performances.1 Despite these challenges, most likely due to her stable live performances gradually dispelled initial skepticism, earning growing recognition for her vocal prowess.

As BABYMONSTER released their first full studio album "DRIP" on November 1, 2024, and their second EP "We Go Up" on October 10, 2025, Pharita continued establishing herself as one of the crucial vocal pillar of the group. Her journey from Bangkok's modeling circuits to becoming one-in-1,226 at YG Entertainment exemplifies the competitive nature of K-pop and the dedication required to succeed in one of the industry's most demanding companies.
