From Child Prodigy to Chart Dominator

IU's debut on September 18, 2008 marked the beginning of one of K-pop's most remarkable careers. Despite early struggles‚Äîher debut single "Lost and Found" failed to chart‚Äîshe persevered through what she later described as the most difficult period of her life.1 By 2010, her duet "Nagging" with 2AM's Seulong became a turning point, ranking second in Gaon Chart's 2010 online sales with over 760 million points.1

Her breakthrough continued with "You and I" in 2011, which topped the newly established Billboard K-Pop Hot 100 chart1‚Äîan unprecedented achievement for a soloist. This dominance caught international attention: in September 2012, Billboard recognized her as the 15th entry on their "21 Under 21: Music's Hottest Minors" list, praising her "unique charm and undeniable hits."2

The Self-Producing Era

After a 17-month hiatus, IU returned in October 2015 with "CHAT-SHIRE"‚Äîa pivotal release that announced her evolution from performer to producer. The EP, with all tracks co-written by IU herself, debuted at the top of local charts.1 Her 2017 album "Palette" cemented this transformation, featuring the chart-dominating "Through the Night" which secured second place on Gaon's 2017 Digital General Top 100 Chart.1

Unprecedented Chart Reign

The 2020s showcased IU's unmatched ability to dominate charts across formats. In May 2020, her collaboration with BTS's Suga, "Eight," achieved global chart success. The music video recorded more than 12 million YouTube views in less than a day after its release.3

Her 2021 album "LILAC" represented her first solo full-length release since "Palette." She placed 16 songs in the top 100 on major music charts, including all 10 tracks of the new album.4 The pre-release track "Celebrity" topped multiple streaming charts, with "Hold My Hand" from 2011 even re-entering at No. 7‚Äîa testament to her deep catalog appeal.4

In 2024, IU made her triumphant return with her sixth EP "The Winning," featuring the pre-release single "Love Wins All" which swiftly ascended to the top of Melon's Top 100 chart within an hour of release.5

Acting: From Drama Darling to Cannes

IU's acting career began alongside her music in 2011 with "Dream High." But it was 2018's "My Mister" that marked her transformation into a serious actress. The tvN drama premiered with the second highest rating of tvN's Wednesday-Thursday lineup6, and despite initial controversy over its premise, concluded with its highest ratings and critical acclaim, topping the weekly TV chart with a CPI score of 249.5.7

In 2022, she made her commercial film debut in Hirokazu Kore-eda's "Broker," premiering at the Cannes Film Festival where co-star Song Kang-ho thanked her in his Best Actor acceptance speech.1 Most recently, her Netflix series "When Life Gives You Tangerines" (March 2025), which reunited her with "My Mister" director Kim Won-suk, received widespread praise and won Best Drama at the 61st Baeksang Arts Awards.8

Record-Breaking World Tour

IU's world tour "H.E.R.," held from March to September 2024, set a remarkable record as the most-attended world tour by a Korean female soloist, drawing 520,000 fans across Asia, Europe and North America.9 In May 2025, she announced her return to music with "Flower Bookmark 3," continuing her signature cover album series nearly eight years after its last installment.9
