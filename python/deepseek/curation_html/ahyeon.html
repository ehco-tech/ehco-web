Early Life and Path to YG Entertainment

Jung Ahyeon was born on April 11, 2007, in Toegye-dong (also referred to as Hupyeong-dong in some sources), Chuncheon, Gangwon-do Province, South Korea. Growing up in the scenic mountainous region of Gangwon, she developed an early passion for performing arts. Her father actively supported her ambitions, capturing videos of her dance performances from the age of five.1

Ahyeon attended Seongrim Elementary School and later Daeryong Middle School in Chuncheon. At age 11, she enrolled at Ara Dance Academy in Chuncheon, where her dedication to refining her craft intensified.2 The academy became a crucial stepping stone, as she began preparing for entertainment company auditions held at the facility. Her multilingual abilities‚Äîhaving started English lessons at five and Chinese at seven‚Äîwould later become significant assets in her idol career.3

In 2017, at just 11 years old, Ahyeon successfully passed the first round of auditions for Banana Culture Entertainment. However, her trajectory changed dramatically in 2018 when she passed auditions for both JYP Entertainment and YG Entertainment.4 She ultimately chose YG Entertainment, joining as a trainee and embarking on a rigorous five-year training period that would shape her into the all-rounder performer she is today. During her trainee years, she attended Hanlim Multi Art School, one of South Korea's most prestigious arts high schools, in the Applied Music Department.5

The Viral "Dangerously" Cover

On April 22, 2023, YG Entertainment uploaded a performance video of Ahyeon covering Charlie Puth's "Dangerously" to BABYMONSTER's official YouTube channel as part of the "Last Evaluation" series.6 The cover immediately captured global attention, showcasing Ahyeon's extraordinary vocal abilities at just 16 years old. Her performance demonstrated seamless transitions between powerful chest voice and delicate falsetto, coupled with impactful high notes and emotional delivery that diverged from the original song's interpretation.7

The video accumulated over 8.74 million views within its first ten days, and the momentum never slowed. Multiple vocal coaches and reaction content creators worldwide analyzed the performance, praising her technical proficiency and stage presence.8 WINNER's Yoon, who served as a judge on "Last Evaluation," remarked that watching Ahyeon felt like witnessing "a power show of her voice."9 Even more remarkably, Yang Hyun-suk, YG Entertainment's founder and executive producer, declared that Ahyeon possessed "the highest vocal range among the seven members" and described her as "the character that best suits the team's name."9

The cover's impact extended beyond view counts. Charlie Puth himself took notice after fans repeatedly asked if he had seen Ahyeon's version. On his TikTok community, Puth left a comment stating "I loved it," sending shockwaves through the K-pop community.8 This interaction eventually led to Puth gifting BABYMONSTER an original composition, "Like That," which was included in their debut mini-album "BABYMONS7ER."10 Yang Hyun-suk later revealed that Puth's gesture was specifically in response to Ahyeon's viral cover, describing it as "a huge gift" for both the members and fans.10 As of late 2024, the "Dangerously" cover has surpassed 39 million views, cementing its status as one of pre-debut K-pop's most legendary performance videos.11

Final Evaluation and Unexpected Hiatus

Throughout early 2023, BABYMONSTER's formation was documented in the reality series "Last Evaluation," which allowed the public to observe the seven potential members competing through monthly evaluations. Ahyeon was the second member officially revealed on January 16, 2023, through a live performance video.12 Her appearances on the show consistently drew praise from industry professionals and senior artists serving as judges.

On May 11, 2023, YG Entertainment announced the final seven-member lineup through a special video on YouTube. Ahyeon ranked first in the final evaluation, solidifying her position as the group's center and affirming her all-rounder capabilities across vocals, rap, and dance.13 The announcement positioned her as one of the most anticipated rookies in K-pop's next generation, with many drawing comparisons to BLACKPINK's Jennie due to her versatile skillset and commanding stage presence‚Äîearning her the pre-debut nickname "Little Jennie."14

However, just days before BABYMONSTER's scheduled November 27, 2023 debut, YG Entertainment made a shocking announcement. On November 15, 2023, the company revealed that Ahyeon would not participate in the debut due to health reasons, and BABYMONSTER would debut as a six-member group.15 The following day, YG clarified that Ahyeon remained a member of the group but would not participate in debut activities, with her future return left uncertain.16 This development sparked widespread concern among fans who had followed her journey through "Last Evaluation," though the company assured the public that her health was the priority.

Triumphant Return and Official Debut

BABYMONSTER proceeded with their debut as planned, releasing "Batter Up" on November 27, 2023, without Ahyeon. While the debut achieved impressive records, fans eagerly anticipated news of Ahyeon's condition. On January 1, 2024, Yang Hyun-suk addressed the situation in a video announcing BABYMONSTER's comeback plans, revealing that Ahyeon had been unable to train for several months due to her health condition and that the timeline for her return remained uncertain.17

The wait finally ended on January 25, 2024, when YG Entertainment released an announcement video confirming Ahyeon's return. The company stated that she had recovered and would rejoin BABYMONSTER for their upcoming first mini-album scheduled for April 1, 2024.18 The announcement also revealed that previously released songs "Batter Up" and "Stuck in the Middle" would be re-recorded to include Ahyeon's vocals, ensuring the complete seven-member sound fans had been anticipating.18

On April 1, 2024, Ahyeon made her official debut with BABYMONSTER as the group released their first mini-album "BABYMONS7ER," featuring the title track "SHEESH."19 Her return generated massive online engagement, with the hashtag #WelcomeBackAhyeon trending globally and amassing millions of interactions within 24 hours of the teaser release.20 The album's success validated the anticipation‚Äîit received over 460,000 pre-orders and debuted at number three on the Circle Album Chart, with "SHEESH" eventually peaking at number ten on the Circle Digital Chart.21

Establishing Her Legacy as an All-Rounder

Following her debut, Ahyeon quickly demonstrated why she had ranked first in the final evaluation. Her role as main vocalist, lead rapper, and lead dancer positioned her as one of K-pop's rare triple threats, a designation shared by only a handful of fourth and fifth-generation idols such as aespa's Karina or LE SSERAFIM's Yunjin.20 Industry observers noted her technically compressed vocal tone and excellent breath control during live performances, qualities that set her apart in an era when live vocal prowess often determines an idol's legitimacy.

Throughout 2024, Ahyeon's performances reinforced her reputation. During BABYMONSTER's comeback stages and fan meeting tour across Asia in May 2024, she showcased solo stages that highlighted her individual artistry. The group's subsequent releases‚Äî"Forever" in July 2024 and their first full album "Drip" in November 2024‚Äîfurther established Ahyeon as a focal point of BABYMONSTER's sound and visual identity.22 The "Drip" music video's success, which became YouTube's most-viewed video within 24 hours upon release, demonstrated the drawing power she and her groupmates had cultivated.

A particularly memorable moment came during the 2025 MAMA Awards on November 29, 2025, where Ahyeon performed alongside fellow members Rora and Pharita as "HUNTR/X"‚Äîcharacters from a Netflix K-pop demon hunter concept. They performed "What It Sounds Like" and "Golden," with critics praising their performance as a "golden peak" that reinforced BABYMONSTER's reputation as a performance powerhouse.23 In January 2025, Ahyeon embarked on BABYMONSTER's first world tour "Hello Monsters," which kicked off with two nights at Seoul's KSPO Dome. The tour would eventually draw 300,000 attendees across 32 dates, establishing the group as major arena-class performers barely a year after their debut.24

Recognition and Future Aspirations

Ahyeon's impact extends beyond performance metrics. Her social media posts consistently generate the highest engagement rates within BABYMONSTER's content, and her styling choices‚Äîfavoring high-waisted jeans, crop tops, and oversized jackets in neutral tones with metallic accents‚Äîhave influenced K-pop fashion trends among younger fans.23 She has appeared in fashion editorials for magazines including Nylon Korea and Dazed Korea, though she has not yet secured solo brand ambassador deals.23

In November 2025, Ahyeon made headlines when it was announced she would not take the 2026 College Scholastic Ability Test despite being in her senior year of high school.25 This decision reflected her full commitment to her idol career, particularly as BABYMONSTER's schedule intensified with their world tour and upcoming releases. On her personal Weverse account in December 2025, Ahyeon shared a vulnerable post stating "I need more confidence," revealing the pressures that accompany being labeled an all-rounder and consistently delivering high notes in demanding performances.23

Despite these pressures, industry recognition continues to accumulate. BABYMONSTER won Rookie Artist of the Year at the 39th Golden Disc Awards in 2025, with Ahyeon expressing that receiving their first award felt like "an unimaginable trophy" in her Weverse update on December 1, 2025.23 As publications like Rolling Stone Korea named BABYMONSTER among 2024's "Future of Music" artists and research firms like Sisa Journal and Gallup Korea designated them as "Next Generation Leaders," Ahyeon's individual trajectory as K-pop's next major solo artist seemed increasingly inevitable.26
